This is a story about Filemon, he is Mexican and lives in Brooklyn, New York.

Filemons is 40 years old and has lives have spent half of his life in New York.

“Well it was March 6, a Monday, and there was a meeting at the church about immigration...”

“...and at 6 o'clock I turned on the TV in my house...”

Televisa: A Mexican woman from Brooklyn was so worried about deportation that she decided to return voluntarily to Mexico

“..well at least the woman decided to go, who was undocumented, with her two daughters, who are American citizens.”

“But the husband stayed.”

“So what happened? It shocked me when I saw that woman because I recognized her from the area, I recognized her from church.”

“This past November Rocío Sanchez returned to Mexico with her daughters out of fear.”

“I have conflicting feelings”

“I feel like its kind of a sad situation, because a lot of familes, a lot of people are dealing with the same thing”

“Well I decided to look for Rocío's husband to cheer him up becasue I figured what is happening is really hard- having half of your family here and half there, thats really sad. That's why I'd like to talk to him.”

On January 20, while Donald Trump took office in Washington D.C., the Filemon's life changed-as did the future for thousands of other undocumented immigrants.

“I don't think t hey are the only ones. I think there are many families that have already gone back like that, that go voluntarily, out of fear. Its such a shame that people have to go like that, not becasue they want to, but kind of forced to. Its hard.”

Filemon's search continues, even if just to find the whereabouts of the Rocío Sanchez's husband-the woman who left out of fear of being deported under the Trump Administration

"It's sad to have someone so ready and talking like that. You have to have courage. To continue or not to keep attracting the community and speaking like he is- seperating families.”
